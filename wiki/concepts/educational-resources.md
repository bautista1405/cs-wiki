# Educational Resources

A curated collection of materials designed to facilitate the learning of Computer Science and Software Engineering.

## Recommended Platforms
- **MOOCs**: Coursera, edX, Udacity, MIT OpenCourseWare (OCW).
- **Interactive Learning**: LeetCode, HackerRank, Exercism.io.
- **Documentation**: MDN Web Docs, official language specifications (e.g., ECMAScript, PEPs for Python).

## Frameworks for Learning
- **Active Recall & Spaced Repetition**: Using tools like Anki for memorizing syntax and theoretical concepts.
- **Project-Based Learning (PBL)**: Building real-world applications to apply theoretical knowledge.
- **Feynman Technique**: Explaining complex concepts in simple terms to identify gaps in understanding.

## Curated Resource Categories
- **Foundational**: Books like \"Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs\" (SICP).
- **Practical**: Tutorials and documentation-driven learning.
- **Advanced**: Academic papers and specialized textbooks.

## Counter-arguments
- Tutorial Hell: The risk of following tutorials without internalizing the "why" behind the code.
- Information Overload: The abundance of resources can lead to paralysis by analysis.
