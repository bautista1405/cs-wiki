# Meta-Learning

Meta-learning is the process of learning how to learn, focusing on the strategies and frameworks that make the acquisition of new knowledge more efficient.

## Learning Strategies

### Project-Based Learning
Learning by doing. Instead of theoretical study, focus on building a functional project. This approach forces the learner to encounter real problems and find practical solutions.
- Sources: [[unknown-2026-project-based-learning]]

### Pattern Recognition
Rather than memorizing individual solutions, focus on identifying underlying patterns that can be applied to a wide range of problems. This is particularly effective in technical domains like algorithmic problem solving.
- Sources: [[xu-gunawardane-2024-coding-interview-patterns]]

### Structured Curriculum and Coaching
Using a formal curriculum and expert coaching (e.g., the Mochary Method) to accelerate leadership and organizational growth. This involves using templates for 1-1s and curated resources for continuous improvement.
- Sources: [[mochary-2026-method-curriculum]]

## Core Philosophy
The shift from "what to learn" to "how to learn" involves moving away from rote memorization and toward a system of patterns, application (projects), and feedback loops (coaching).

## Bias Check
- **Counter-arguments**: An over-emphasis on patterns can lead to "pattern matching" where a learner applies a familiar solution to a problem it doesn't actually fit, ignoring the nuances of the specific case.
- **Data gaps**: There is limited information on how to transition from a beginner (project-based) to an expert (pattern-based) learner.
