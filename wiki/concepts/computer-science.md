# Computer Science

The study of computers and computational systems. Unlike electronics engineering or information systems, CS focuses primarily on software and software systems. This field both studies and produces software to be used in computing.

## Major Sub-fields
- **Theoretical Computer Science**: Analysis of algorithms, complexity theory, cryptography, and information theory.
- **Systems**: Operating systems, distributed systems, compiler design, and embedded systems.
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**: Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, Robotics, and Neural Networks.
- **Software Engineering**: Methodology for the systematic development, operation, and maintenance of software.
- **Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)**: The design and implementation of user interfaces.

## Core Concepts
- **Abstraction**: Hiding complexity to simplify interaction.
- **Turing Completeness**: The ability of a system of computation to simulate any other Turing machine.
- **Complexity**: How resource requirements (time/space) grow as input size increases.

## History & Evolution
- From mechanical calculators $\rightarrow$ Vacuum tubes $\rightarrow$ Transistors $\rightarrow$ Integrated Circuits $\rightarrow$ Cloud/Quantum Computing.

## Counter-arguments
- The definition of "CS" is often conflated with "Programming," whereas programming is a tool used within CS.
