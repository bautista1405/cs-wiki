# Summary
A curated list of software engineering "laws" (e.g., Brooks's Law, Conway's Law, Hyrum's Law) that describe systemic patterns in software development and team dynamics.

## Key Concepts
- **Conway's Law**: Organizations which design systems are constrained to produce designs which are copies of the communication structures of these organizations.
- **Brooks's Law**: Adding manpower to a late software project makes it later.
- **Hyrum's Law**: With a sufficient number of users of an API, it does not matter what you promise in the contract: all observable behaviors of your system will be depended on by somebody.

## Bias Checks
- **Counter-arguments**: "Laws" in software engineering are often heuristics or observations, not mathematical certainties; they can be mitigated by specific organizational practices.
- **Data gaps**: Most laws are qualitative observations from the 60s-90s; modern DevOps and Agile may change some of these dynamics.
