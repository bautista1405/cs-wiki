# Lessons from Starting, Building, and Exiting a DevTools Startup (2024)

## Summary
Insights from Boris Tane on the realities of building a developer-focused company, emphasizing Product Market Fit (PMF) and agility.

## Key Takeaways
- PMF is the primary goal; avoid premature optimization of architecture and clean code.
- "Better, faster, cheaper" is insufficient; look for technological shifts to create a monopoly.
- Manual, non-scalable work is essential for early growth.
- Hype is a necessary tool for public awareness.
- Maintaining a side hobby project preserves the love for building.

## Bias Check
- **Counter-arguments**: The "ignore clean code" advice may lead to massive technical debt that hinders scaling once PMF is found.
- **Data gaps**: Perspectives are limited to the devtools niche and a single founder's experience.
