# Coding Interview Patterns

Mastering coding interviews requires the ability to recognize algorithmic patterns and map problems to these patterns rather than memorizing individual solutions.

## The 19 Essential Patterns

### Linear/Array Patterns
1. **Two Pointers**: Searching pairs in sorted arrays or validating palindromes.
2. **Hash Maps and Sets**: Constant-time lookups and frequency counting.
3. **Fast and Slow Pointers**: Cycle detection and finding midpoints.
4. **Sliding Windows**: Contiguous subarrays or substrings.
5. **Prefix Sums**: Efficiently calculating sums of subarrays.

### Search and Sort
6. **Binary Search**: Optimization for searching in sorted data.
7. **Sort and Search**: Combining sorting with searching techniques.

### Data Structure Specifics
8. **Linked Lists**: Pointer manipulation and structural changes.
9. **Stacks**: Nested structures and order of operations.
10. **Heaps**: Tracking Top-K elements or merging sorted lists.
11. **Tries**: Word searches and prefix matching.
12. **Trees**: Recursive traversals and hierarchical structures.
13. **Graphs**: Connectivity, traversal (BFS/DFS), and shortest paths.

### Algorithmic Strategies
14. **Intervals**: Managing ranges and overlaps.
15. **Backtracking**: Exploring permutations and combinations.
16. **Dynamic Programming (DP)**: Overlapping subproblems and optimal substructure.
17. **Greedy Algorithms**: Locally optimal choices at each step.
18. **Bit Manipulation**: Low-level operations for optimization.
19. **Math and Geometry**: Coordinate planes, GCD, and overflow handling.

## Implementation Tips
- **Complexity Analysis**: Always analyze Time and Space complexity (Big O).
- **Edge Case Handling**: Be mindful of integer overflow and use GCD for slopes in geometry to avoid precision issues.
