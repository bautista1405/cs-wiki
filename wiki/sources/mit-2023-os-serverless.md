# Summary: MIT 6.5810 Serverless Computing Seminar

A graduate-level seminar focused on research in [[operating-systems]], specifically specializing in serverless computing for the Fall 2023 term.

## Course Scope
The seminar explores the architecture, implementation, and optimization of serverless platforms. It transitions through several key themes:
- **Programming Serverless**: Frameworks like Kappa and Boki for stateful serverless computing.
- **Container Implementation**: Lightweight virtualization using Firecracker and gVisor.
- **Running Containers**: On-demand loading and workload characterization in cloud providers (AWS Lambda).
- **Storage & Resource Utilization**: Ephemeral storage (Pocket) and memory offloading (TMO).
- **Fast Start/Low Latency**: Distributed futures (Ownership) and RDMA-codesigned remote forks.

## Academic Context
The course assumes prior knowledge of undergraduate OS (6.1810) or distributed systems (6.5840), emphasizing a research-driven approach where students lead discussions on academic papers.

## Counter-arguments
- The course is heavily skewed toward "serverless" research; may lack depth in traditional monolithic kernel architecture which is still prevalent in many industrial systems.
