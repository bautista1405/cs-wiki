# Teaching Methodologies (DSA)

The design of Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) curricula varies between academic, curated, and practical industry-focused approaches.

## Academic Approach
- **Structured Sequence**: Moving from basics (Lists, Stacks) to complex structures (B-Trees) and then algorithms (Dijkstra) [[uiuc-2023-cs225]].
- **Implementation Focus**: Strong emphasis on memory management (Stack vs Heap) and language-specific features like Templates and Inheritance in C++ [[uiuc-2023-cs225]].
- **Assessment**: Reliance on labs and exams to validate theoretical and practical understanding [[uiuc-2023-cs225]].

## Curated/Resource-Driven Approach
- **Aggregation**: Providing "awesome lists" that link to multiple high-quality external sources (e.g., Princeton, Rice University) [[tayllan-2026-awesome-algorithms]].
- **Concentrated Review**: Using cheat sheets and algorithmic cards for rapid review and interview preparation [[tayllan-2026-awesome-algorithms]].

## Bias and Gaps
- **Pedagogical Gap**: Academic paths may lack focus on modern hardware optimization (HPC), while curated lists may lack the structured guidance and rigorous testing of a formal course [[uiuc-2023-cs225], [tayllan-2026-awesome-algorithms]].
- **Static vs. Dynamic**: Course-based learning is often tied to a specific semester (e.g., Spring 2023), whereas curated lists are more easily updated but less cohesive [[uiuc-2023-cs225]].
