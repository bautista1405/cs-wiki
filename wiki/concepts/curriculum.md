# Curriculum

A structured sequence of learning objectives designed to lead a student from basic to advanced proficiency in Computer Science.

## Core Pillars of a CS Curriculum
1. **Mathematics**: Discrete Math, Linear Algebra, Calculus, Statistics.
2. **Programming Fundamentals**: Syntax, Control Flow, Functions, OOP, Functional Programming.
3. **Systems**: Operating Systems, Computer Architecture, Networking, Databases.
4. **Theory**: Automata, Complexity Theory, Algorithms, Data Structures.
5. **Software Engineering**: Design Patterns, Testing, CI/CD, Version Control, Agile.

## Approaches to Curriculum Design
- **Top-Down**: Starting with a high-level project and drilling down into the necessary concepts.
- **Bottom-Up**: Starting with binary/logic gates and building up to high-level languages.
- **Spiral**: Revisiting the same topics with increasing complexity over time.

## Essential Milestones
- Hello World $\rightarrow$ Simple Apps $\rightarrow$ Data Structures $\rightarrow$ System Design $\rightarrow$ Contributions to Open Source.

## Counter-arguments
- Rigid curricula may lag behind industry trends (e.g., AI/ML integration).
- Standardized curricula may ignore the unique learning speed of individuals.
