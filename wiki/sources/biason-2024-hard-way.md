# Summary
A collection of personal reflections and hard-won lessons from real-world software development projects. Covers practical aspects of maintenance, tooling, and teamwork.

## Key Concepts
- **Simplicity Over Cleverness**: The high cost of "clever" code in terms of maintainability and onboarding.
- **Testing as a Safety Net**: The necessity of comprehensive testing not just for correctness, but for the confidence to refactor.
- **Incremental Changes**: The value of small, atomic commits and deployments over large, risky releases.

## Bias Checks
- **Counter-arguments**: These are anecdotal experiences; what worked "the hard way" for one engineer may not be a universal law.
- **Data gaps**: Lacks quantitative data to support the qualitative claims.
