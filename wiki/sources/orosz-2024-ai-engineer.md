# Summary
Analysis of the shift in the engineering landscape as AI becomes integrated into the development lifecycle. Discusses the skill set required for the "AI Engineer" role versus a traditional Software Engineer or ML Researcher.

## Key Concepts
- **AI Engineering vs. ML Research**: AI engineers focus on applying existing models (via APIs and orchestration frameworks like LangChain) rather than training new architectures.
- **New Skill Set**: Prompt engineering, vector databases, RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation), and evaluation frameworks.
- **Tooling Shift**: The move towards AI-assisted coding and the changing nature of software requirements.

## Bias Checks
- **Counter-arguments**: The "AI Engineer" role might be a transient phase as AI capabilities become standard features of all software engineering roles.
- **Data gaps**: Based largely on current market trends; long-term career trajectories for AI Engineers are still speculative.
