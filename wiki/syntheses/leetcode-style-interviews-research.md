# Passing LeetCode-Style Live Coding Interviews

Passing a live coding interview requires a dual-track approach: **Technical Mastery** and **Communication Excellence**.

## 1. Technical Track: Pattern-Based Learning
The most effective way to prepare is not by solving hundreds of random problems, but by mastering **algorithmic patterns**. 
- **Pattern Recognition**: As detailed in [[pattern-recognition]], the goal is to recognize a problem as a 'Sliding Window' or 'DFS' problem within seconds.
- **Iterative Improvement**: Start with a [[brute-force-solution]], analyze its [[complexity-analysis]], and move towards the optimal approach through [[iterative-refinement]].
- **The Roadmap**: A structured path (like NeetCode 150) ensures no major category (graphs, trees, DP) is missed.

## 2. Communication Track: Thinking Aloud
The technical solution is only half the battle. The interviewer evaluates the *process*.
- **Thinking Aloud**: Continuous narration (see [[thinking-aloud]]) prevents silence and allows the interviewer to provide hints.
- **Collaboration**: Treat the session as pair-programming.
- **Mental Resilience**: Use specific strategies for [[handling-mistakes]] to show resilience and debugging skills.

## 3. Validation Track: Mock Practice
Bridge the gap between 'solving in a bedroom' and 'solving in a meeting'.
- Use platforms like Pramp for peer mocks.
- Focus on timing and articulating the la-ha moment.

## Conclusion
A successful candidate is one who can:
1. Correctly identify the pattern.
2. Communicate a brute-force approach and its complexity.
3. Iteratively refine to the optimal solution while thinking aloud.
4. Recover gracefully from mistakes.
